KEPLER 452 b

Is it habitable?

It is the first potentially rocky super-Earth planet discovered orbiting within the habitable zone of a very Sun-like star. However, it is unknown if it is entirely habitable

Environment climate

Land and fertile

However, it's essential to note that Kepler-452b receives about 10% more energy from its star than Earth receives from the Sun, which could lead to a runaway greenhouse effect ². This effect might impact the planet's habitability and fertility.

Assumed Gas Components

1. Nitrogen (N2): 50-80%
2. Oxygen (O2): 10-30%
3. Carbon dioxide (CO2): 5-15%
4. Water vapor (H2O): 5-10%
5. Methane (CH4): 1-5%
6. Argon (Ar): 1-2%
7. Helium (He): 0.5-1%

Constellation sorrounding

Kepler-452b is located in the constellation Cygnus, about 1,400 light-years away from Earth ¹ ². To give you a better idea, Cygnus is a beautiful swan-shaped constellation in the northern sky, named after the Latin word for "swan." It's also home to many other fascinating celestial objects, including the North America Nebula and the X-ray binary star system, Cygnus X-1.

Sky looks like

Kepler-452b's sky would be vastly different from Earth's due to its unique location and atmospheric conditions. Here's a hypothetical representation:

Daytime Sky:
1. Color: Deep blue to violet, due to the star's G-type spectrum (similar to the Sun).
2. Brightness: About 10% brighter than Earth's sky.
3. Star appearance: Kepler-452, the parent star, would appear 1.3 times larger than our Sun.

Nighttime Sky:

1. Constellations: Unrecognizable from Earth's constellations due to Kepler-452b's different vantage point.
2. Star density: More stars visible due to the planet's location in the Milky Way's galactic plane.
3. Planetary visibility: Nearby planets in the Kepler-452 system would be visible, potentially with unique configurations.

Structure

Kepler-452b's structure is inferred based on its size, mass, and orbital characteristics.

Aurora Characteristics

Color: Vibrant green, blue, and red hues due to excited oxygen and nitrogen atoms.
-Pattern Undulating curtains, swirling vortex-like structures, and diffuse glows.
-Intensity: Brighter than Earth's aurora borealis (northern lights) due to Kepler-452b's stronger magnetic field.
- Frequency: Occasional, coinciding with solar flares and coronal mass ejections.

Magnetic Field Properties

-Strength: 2-5 times Earth's magnetic field.
-Configuration: Dipolar, with a tilted axis.
-Reconnection Events: Frequent, leading to spectacular aurora displays.
